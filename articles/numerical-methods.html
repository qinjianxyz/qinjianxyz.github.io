Numerical Methods in Modern Engineering

With increasing computational power, there comes increasing possibilities to tackle familiar problems with novel perspectives and creative techniques. Numerical method is one of many products of modern computational power, and it has become an essential component of scientific computing. This article introduces what numerical methods are and give some practical applications of it. The central idea of numerical method is to convert complex and often intractable analytical problems into solvable mathematical models to find an approximate solution to an arbitrary degree of accuracy.

There are many practical applications in numerical methods, such as finite element analysis, numerical integration, etc. We will look into root finding first. For example, try to solve this simple question: x^x = ln(x), what is the value x, assume x is a real number? (^ means power, 2^3 = 2*2*2 = 8). One might quickly realize that this innocent looking equation is almost impossible to solve analytically. However, with a numerical technique called bisection, one can get a very close answer to the approximate value of the irrational solution to this equation. There are many numerical methods to solve this problem and bisection is just one of them.

Numerical methods play a vital role in modern engineering. Before computational power becomes an essential part of scientific or engineering research, if one wants to model the air flow through the wings of an airplane, there can be pages of complicated equations and derivations trying to incorporate every detail caused by the nuanced interactions between the wings and their surrounding air. There will be lift force, air drag, vibrations of wings, etc. But finite element analysis (FEA) has given engineers a new perspective to look at this problem. The idea of finite element analysis is to take a large component and break it down into infinitely small nodes connected by meshes. The effect of one node would be reflected by its neighbors and if the computational power allows us to have nodes close enough, we can get very accurate results. Much of this is done by matrix calculations, and the idea of FEA has been proposed long before today. But due to limitations of computational power in the past, no one can go through all of the matrix calculations by hand and obtain useful results from them. If we look at modern CAD software, Autodesk Inventor, Dassault Syst√®mes Solidworks, etc., all of their simulations are conducted by numerical methods instead of analytical methods. Finite element analysis is a simple yet sobering technique. Simplicity does not mean that the implementation is easy. On the contrary, the difficult part of FEA is during the setup of the problem. Engineers need to set the geometry, meshes, constraints, physical parameters properly so the model could work. Optimized meshes for arbitrary geometry is still an open problem that many researchers and industry professionals work on. In fact, a new field is emerging called computational science, whose core idea is to utilize mathematical models to solve complex problems. I believe in the upcoming decades this field would render tremendously valuable services.

What fascinates me the most about numerical method is not about solving integration or find the stress and strain relationships of a complex structure. These are definitely elegant applications, but what interests me the most is that numerical methods a new way of thinking about engineering problems. The big lesson I have learned from numerical methods is that we could break down a complex problem into arbitrarily small problems and solve them bottom up. This is like the divide and conquer in sorting algorithm which has made computing so much more efficient. There is so much more practical use of numerical methods, and this article only gives a brief introduction of the topic. The increasingly popular field of machine learning is built on numerical methods. It is a way of thinking that scientists and engineers should look into, and I believe the proper incorporation of numerical methods of any field would render useful services.
