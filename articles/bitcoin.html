Bitcoin Does Not Work; Alternatives Could

People like me have always wanted a truly free currency where the meaning of money is the in the simplest form of transaction and exchange of goods and services. The U.S. dollar is the closest international currency to accomplish this goal because the economic power of the U.S. and the U.S. dollar is also backed by the strongest military in the world. Unfortunately, the complex financial system makes this simple dream nearly impossible. Almost every transaction is taxed and monitored. There is nothing intrinsically wrong of tracking transactions. But it is difficult to persuade that the ice cream sale at the street corner really has something to do with the latest military equipment in a foreign military base. I believe the emergence of bitcoin is caused by such fundamental and fervent desires to just make free transactions. But the truth is that at this stage, Bitcoin is not functional either as a currency or an investment. In this article, I would discuss the challenges Bitcoin faces and how future technology could achieve its promises if the global governments ever allow it to happen.

There are several reasons why bitcoin is not a good investment if it counts as investment at all. The most prominent reason is that there is no intrinsic value of profits or production of any goods or services coming out of bitcoin. It is a slightly different case to use bitcoin as a currency because every single currency has value only because people believe it has value. But in terms of investment, people who are rushing into the market are only expecting that someone else is willing to buy the bitcoin at a higher price than when they bought it. There is no free money like that in the world especially when something literally does not produce any profits.

The second most important reason why bitcoin does not work as an investment is because many people are trading derivatives of bitcoin instead of the real bitcoin using blockchain. For example, let us consider the popular trading platform Robinhood, which recently has allowed its customers to purchase bitcoin on its platform. If anyone purchases bitcoin through Robinhood, the person is not getting a real bitcoin with a public key and private key pair to access in the blockchain. From my perspective, derivative trading for bitcoin goes against every founding principle of a decentralized currency and it is destined to fail.

After looking at two compelling reasons why we should not use bitcoin as an investment, let us consider a slightly different question: can we use bitcoin as a mainstream currency then? There are many problems with using bitcoin as a main currency. To begin with, bitcoin can be very easily used for distortion and scam and money laundering. After all, we are talking about a currency that is completely anonymous and I am not sure people would appreciate this fact as much when a large scale of criminal activities happen. Furthermore, if anyone wants to trade bitcoin in the United States, it is likely that their identity needs to be verified through their social security. This fact actually makes me want to laugh because if bitcoin is completely monitored by the government, it has no difference from any other currency. You could pay bitcoin to buy a tesla all you want, but the government is still able to tax those transactions. The entire system is rendered useless under current format.

The most important downside of using bitcoin as a currency is its unstable money supply which leads to massive electricity usage from bitcoin mining in the world. Bitcoin mining put in simple terms is just to reverse an intrinsically irreversible process related to hash functions. If one could find the input that creates the desired output, the person would be rewarded with an amount of bitcoin. Frankly, it is an interesting concept because ultimately a higher computational power is rewarded with money. But as mentioned earlier, such method takes massive amount of electricity and frankly it is a waste of resources. Also, it seems arbitrary that money should go to people with the most computational power. The most ridiculous thing is that there is a fixed amount of bitcoins in the world, and it just does not work as a long term money supply.

If a decentralized currency should work in the future to make payments both secure and free, it must answer the above concerns and have thoughtful and thorough designs. In the current cryptocurrency market, it is mainly a group of people trying to manipulate and take advantage of others, and others have no idea what is going on. Such phenomenon would do more harm than good to the blockchain technology in the short term because people would quickly regard it as a scam. The truth is blockchain is not a scam; it is a immature technology that is misunderstood and abused by many people. I hope to witness a functional and decentralized international currency one day.
