10 Questions

What people ask before they bring me in.

I'm not technical. Will I understand what you're doing?

Yes — that's the point. I explain every decision in plain language and show you working software instead of jargon. You'll always know what's being built, why, and what it costs. And you don't need a perfectly-diagnosed "AI problem" to start — if you can describe what's slowing you down, we can work together. I'll tell you straight whether it's even an AI problem.

What does this cost, and how do you work?

I price per project, not per hour — most work runs as a fixed-scope sprint (typically 2 to 8 weeks) with a flat price agreed up front, so you know the number before any work begins. There's no open-ended meter. The first call is free and includes a rough ballpark so you can decide with real information.

You have no client testimonials yet. Why should I trust you?

Honestly: because I'd rather show you real, checkable work than invent reviews. Anvil Sim is live right now — a complete AI product I built and shipped alone. I've also taken AI to production for 10M+ monthly users. And because I'm taking on a small number of first projects, we can start with a small paid pilot: if the first milestone doesn't convince you, we stop there — no long contract on faith.

How is this different from hiring an agency or a consultant?

Most people selling AI help can either operate at scale or build the whole thing solo — rarely both. I've done both: production systems at 10M-user scale, and a complete AI product shipped alone. Consultants hand you a strategy deck and disappear before any code exists; agencies bill for a team you never meet and have to manage. I'm one senior engineer who diagnoses the real problem and then builds and ships it myself.

Will I be locked into something only you can maintain?

No. I hand back work that's documented, tested, and maintainable, so your team can own and extend it after I'm gone. De-risking the handover is part of the job — you're never left with a black box you can't change.

Would an off-the-shelf tool be cheaper than hiring you?

Sometimes, yes — and I'll tell you when it is. If an existing product does the job, that's the honest recommendation and it saves you money. I build custom when your data, workflow, or quality bar means the off-the-shelf version won't cut it. Knowing when to buy instead of build is part of giving you a straight answer.

Do you only do AI work?

AI is the focus, but I build the whole product around it when you need it — interface, backend, data, and infrastructure — as one person, so you get a coherent working thing instead of a pile of parts to integrate.
