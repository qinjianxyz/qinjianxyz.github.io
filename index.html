How I choose tools

Tool-agnostic.
Outcome-specific.

I do not start by forcing AI into the problem. The useful answer might be software, a model, a dashboard, a process change, an experiment, or hands-on work with the system itself.

My job is to find the blocker and move it—not defend a favorite technology.

Where I am unusually useful

Problems that cross boundaries.

Breadth matters when the real issue sits between teams, tools, or disciplines.

Software + AI

A manual workflow needs to become a dependable system.

Agents, integrations, internal tools, data pipelines, product engineering, tests, monitoring, and human review.

Data + decisions

The information exists, but nobody can reliably act on it.

Research systems, dashboards, source pipelines, decision records, technical reviews, and evidence-backed recommendations.

Software + the physical world

The model must meet geometry, hardware, motion, heat, or real operators.

Engineering simulation, computer vision, robotics, validation plans, instrumentation, and prototype-to-production architecture.

Ownership + execution

Everyone sees part of the problem, but no one owns the whole result.

Cross-functional diagnosis, practical prioritization, build-vs-buy decisions, operating systems, and senior hands-on leadership.
