Work Experience
Computational Fluid Dynamics Research

Currently conducting research on computation fluid dynamics. The research aims to understand how different shapes of an object affects its flight path by using numerical method and finite element method. The research studies a special case of the arbitray Langrangian Eulerian (ALE) formulation for rigid bodies. The mesh, nodes, pdes are generated and solved in a software by fundamental physical equations such as the Navier Stokes equation. Upon completion, the feature of the research is the ability to import arbitrary 3D shapes created by most commercial CAD software to achieve universal low-mach number rigid body flight path simulation.
